📈 S1 — RSI Momentum Strategy
Uses the Relative Strength Index (RSI) to identify momentum breakouts. RSI measures the speed and magnitude of recent price changes on a scale of 0–100. Readings above 60 signal strong bullish momentum; below 40 signals weakness. This is the original strategy — battle-tested on Nifty 50 stocks and index options using 15-min candles.
🟢 BUY Signal
RSI crosses above 60 (from below). Indicates building momentum — enter a long position at market price. Trailing SL activates immediately.
🔵 Recovery BUY
After a sell, if RSI drops below 40 then crosses above 42, a post-dip recovery entry is triggered. Lower-risk re-entry after momentum returns.
🔴 SELL Signal
RSI drops below 40 (momentum reversal) or trailing SL is hit. Whichever comes first closes the position.
DEFAULT PARAMETERS
ParameterDefaultDescription
RSI Period14Lookback candles for RSI calculation
Buy Threshold60RSI level that triggers a BUY
Sell Threshold40RSI level that triggers a SELL
Recovery42Post-dip re-entry threshold
Trailing SL20%SL trails at X% below highest price
💡 Best for: Trending Nifty 50 stocks and NIFTY/BANKNIFTY index with clear momentum breakouts. Works well during morning sessions (9:30–11:30) and high-volume days. Avoid during choppy sideways markets.
📊 S2 — EMA(44) + Bollinger Bands Strategy
Combines a 44-period Exponential Moving Average (EMA) as a trend filter with Bollinger Bands (BB) to detect volatility expansions. The EMA tells you the trend direction; BB tells you when price is at an extreme — together they find high-probability entries with built-in volatility context.
🟢 BUY Signal
Price is above EMA(44) (uptrend confirmed) AND price has touched or fallen to the Lower Bollinger Band (pullback to value). Price must then close back above the lower band — a mean-reversion entry within an uptrend.
🔴 SELL Signal
Price touches or closes above the Upper Bollinger Band (overbought extension) OR price crosses below EMA(44) (trend reversal). Exit on whichever fires first.
📐 Indicator Formulas
EMA(44): Exponential average with α = 2/(44+1). Recent prices weighted more heavily.
BB Upper: SMA(20) + 2 × StdDev(20)
BB Lower: SMA(20) − 2 × StdDev(20)
BB Width: Narrow bands = low volatility (squeeze) → explosive move incoming
ParameterDefaultDescription
EMA Period44Trend-following EMA period
BB Period20SMA period for Bollinger Bands
BB Std Dev2.0Band width multiplier (1.5–2.5 typical)
Trailing SL18%Percentage trailing stop loss
💡 Best for: Options trading (NIFTY CE/PE) and trending Nifty stocks. The EMA(44) aligns well with daily-to-weekly swing moves. Particularly powerful when BB squeeze precedes a breakout.
⚡ S3 — MACD Crossover Strategy
The Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD) measures momentum by comparing two EMAs. When the MACD line crosses the signal line, it indicates a shift in trend momentum. A classic trend-following strategy suited to medium-term moves in both equities and index options.
🟢 BUY Signal
MACD line crosses above the Signal line (histogram turns positive). Indicates accelerating bullish momentum. The further below zero this crossover occurs, the stronger the signal.
🔴 SELL Signal
MACD line crosses below the Signal line (histogram turns negative) OR trailing SL is hit.
📐 Formula
MACD Line: EMA(12) − EMA(26)
Signal Line: EMA(9) of MACD line
Histogram: MACD − Signal (shows divergence speed)
ParameterDefaultDescription
MACD Fast12Faster EMA period
MACD Slow26Slower EMA period (trend baseline)
Signal Period9Smoothing for signal line
Trailing SL22%Slightly wider for MACD (slower signals)
💡 Best for: Index options with 1–5 day holding periods. MACD reacts slightly slower than RSI, reducing false signals in choppy markets.
🔬 S4 — RSI + MACD Combined Strategy
The most selective strategy — requires dual confirmation from both RSI (momentum gauge) and MACD (trend momentum) before entering a trade. Fewer signals but significantly higher quality. The RSI filters premature MACD crossovers, and MACD filters RSI spikes in ranging markets.
🟢 BUY (Both must confirm)
Condition 1: RSI crosses above the buy threshold (default 55)
Condition 2: MACD is above signal line (histogram positive)
Both must be true simultaneously.
🔴 SELL (Either triggers exit)
Condition 1 (OR): RSI drops below the sell threshold (default 45)
Condition 2 (OR): MACD crosses below signal line
Trailing SL also exits the position.
WHY COMBINED IS POWERFUL
S4 trades less frequently (~40% fewer signals than S1 or S3 alone), but each trade has higher conviction since two independent indicators agree. Backtesting typically shows improved win rate by 10–20% compared to using either indicator alone.
💡 Best for: High-value NIFTY options and blue-chip Nifty 50 stocks. Ideal when you want fewer, higher-quality trades.
🎯 S5 — VWAP + Stochastic Strategy
Combines the Volume Weighted Average Price (VWAP) — the institutional benchmark price — with the Stochastic Oscillator, a momentum indicator that compares a closing price to its range over a given period. VWAP defines the fair value zone; Stochastic identifies oversold/overbought extremes within that context. Especially powerful for intraday index trading.
📐 VWAP Explained
VWAP = Σ(Price × Volume) / ΣVolume
It resets each trading day. Price above VWAP = buyers in control. Price below VWAP = sellers dominating. Institutions use VWAP as a benchmark — buying below and selling above is considered favorable.
VWAP Bands = VWAP ± (deviation × rolling std dev). Price touching lower band = deep pullback; upper band = extended.
📐 Stochastic Explained
%K = (Close − Lowest Low) / (Highest High − Lowest Low) × 100
%D = SMA(3) of %K — the signal line
Ranges 0–100. Below 20 = oversold (potential buy). Above 80 = overbought (potential sell).
The crossover of %K above %D in oversold territory is a high-conviction buy signal.
🟢 BUY Signal
Price is above VWAP (institutional support) AND Stochastic %K is in oversold zone (<20) and crosses above %D (momentum turning up). This finds pullback entries in an overall bullish day.
🔴 SELL Signal
Price crosses below VWAP (bearish flip) OR Stochastic %K crosses above 80 and starts declining below %D (overbought reversal). Trailing SL also exits.
